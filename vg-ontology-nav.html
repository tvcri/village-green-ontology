Village Green — Ontology

Working model of the entities, roles, and relationships in VG's operational domain. Current scope: member and volunteer onboarding. Service requests are the next major domain.

Person

Core identity
  • Names — first, middle initial, last, preferred/nickname
  • Preferred pronouns
  • Date of birth · gender · veteran status
  • Preferred language (single value)
  • Languages spoken (set)
  • Street address
  • Contact methods — landline, cell, email (typed, multiple)
  • Accessibility needs — hearing, vision, mobility
  • Emergency contacts (structured sub-entities, 1..n)
  • lgl_constituent_id — CRM sync hook

User

Role
  • Username · credential / auth info
  • Access level (includes staff)

Member

Role
  • Status history — VG is authority
  • Circle of Pride flags
  • Newsletter · invoice delivery preferences
  • Dues arrangement
  • Service eligibility status

Volunteer

Role
  • Status history — VG is authority
  • Service capabilities (set)
  • Circle of Pride flag
  • Employment status · occupation
  • Friendship visit interests (free text, optional)
  • Driving credentials validated flag

Household

Grouping
  • Member 1 · Member 2 (references)
  • Dues level · payment method · frequency

Emergency contact

Owned by Person
  • Name · relationship
  • Phone(s) · email (optional)

Pending member

Transient pre-role
  • Application date
  • Ambassador (reference)
  • Household type — single / dual
  • Ambassador notes (retrospective, free text)
  • Waiver signed · signature date
  • Dues level selected · payment method
  • Dues requirement (owned, exactly 1)
  • Graduation event reference
  • Rejection / withdrawal event reference

Pending volunteer

Transient pre-role
  • Application date
  • Ambassador (reference)
  • Capabilities expressed interest in
  • Waiver signed · signature date
  • Background check — auth date, completion, outcome
  • Orientation — date, format (Zoom / in-person)
  • Badge — photo received, lanyard preference
  • Vehicle records (if driver capability selected)
  • Onboarding requirements (owned, 1..n)
  • Graduation / withdrawal event reference

Dues requirement

Owned by Pending member
  • Status — outstanding / satisfied / waived
  • Satisfied date
  • Dues evidence (owned, 1..n)
Dues evidence
  • Evidence type — attestation / transaction
  • Recorded by (staff User reference)
  • Recorded date
  • Transaction reference (if type = transaction)
  • Notes (optional)

Onboarding requirement

Owned by Pending volunteer
  • Requirement type
  • Triggering capability
  • Status — outstanding / satisfied / waived
  • Satisfied date
  • Onboarding evidence (owned, 1..n)
Onboarding evidence
  • Evidence type — attestation / document
  • Recorded by (staff User reference)
  • Recorded date
  • Document reference (if type = document)
  • Notes (optional)
May 2026
Person as roles container
Adopted roles pattern (User, Member, Volunteer as separate entities keyed by pId) rather than flags on Person. A person can hold multiple roles simultaneously or sequentially; role-specific data stays with the role.
May 2026
Pending as pre-role, not state
PendingMember and PendingVolunteer are distinct transient entities, not status values on Member/Volunteer. A pending person is ontologically different — they have not yet been granted the role. Conflating them would blur the graduation boundary.
May 2026
Pending records persist permanently
Graduation is an event, not a transformation. A new role record is created at graduation; the pending record is closed and preserved as the audit trail for how a person entered the system, including rejection and withdrawal outcomes.
May 2026
Ambassador visit is pre-entry, not in-system
The Membership Ambassador visit clears before a PendingMember record is created. VG does not track the visit as a workflow step. Ambassador notes on PendingMember are retrospective documentation only.
May 2026
Language on Person, not role
Both preferredLanguage and languagesSpoken placed on Person. Language is a fact about the person relevant to service matching (volunteer side) and communication preferences (member side) — it should not be duplicated or omitted depending on which role surface is used.
May 2026
Emergency contacts structured on Person
Modeled as structured sub-entities rather than free text. However rarely used, the operational need requires actionable data (a phone number, not a note). Placed on Person because the information does not vary by role.
May 2026
Requirement vs. evidence separation
In both the member and volunteer contexts, the fact of clearance is separated from the means of clearance. DuesRequirement / DuesEvidence for members; OnboardingRequirement / OnboardingEvidence for volunteers. Allows each evidence model to evolve independently — currently staff attestation, potentially document blobs or external transaction references in future.
May 2026
Household is thin
Considered richer Household model covering shared address, communications, and events. Rejected. Household exists solely as a billing convenience linking two Members to a shared dues arrangement.
May 2026
Dynamic onboarding checklist over fixed pipeline
PendingVolunteer onboarding modeled as a generated set of OnboardingRequirements rather than a fixed stage pipeline. Requirement set varies by capability selection, and real-world onboarding involves back-and-forth reentry that a linear pipeline would misrepresent.
May 2026
LGL event model rejected for Village events
LGL's event model is fundraising-oriented (donation attribution with a date). Unsuitable for Village community events. Events domain deferred to a future dedicated system. VG's stable pId on Person is sufficient future-proofing for event registration foreign keys.
